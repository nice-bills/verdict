The problem

Markets are easy to launch. Trusting the outcome is not.

Creating a market takes minutes. Deciding who was right still means committees, votes, or opaque bots — and that limits what you can build on top.

Why it breaks

Every resolver picks speed or trust — rarely both.

Human committees

Days to weeks

Subjective, costly, and hard to compose with automated agents.

DAO / multisig

Governance drag

Small markets rarely justify the coordination cost.

Centralized bots

Fast but opaque

No shared standard for reasoning or audit trails.

The shift

Somnia turns resolution into a verifiable service.

Agents do the reading. Validators reach consensus. Contracts pay out only after an attested response — with reasoning you can inspect.
