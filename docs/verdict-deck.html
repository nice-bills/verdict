The problem

Oracle trust is the bottleneck for on-chain prediction markets

Markets are easy to create. Deciding who was right is slow, political, or centralized — and that kills composability.

Why incumbents fail

Every resolver trades speed for trust — or the reverse

Human committees

Days to weeks

Subjective, expensive, doesn't scale to agent swarms

Multisig / DAO vote

Governance drag

Whales coordinate; small markets aren't worth the vote

Trusted bots

Single point of failure

"Trust me bro" APIs — opaque, unverifiable reasoning

The shift

Somnia's agentic L1 turns resolution into a verifiable service

LLM agents execute off-chain work. Validator consensus anchors the result. Smart contracts only pay out after a cryptographically attested response.
